Long Island Youth Martial Arts

Long Island has an exceptional martial arts community spanning traditional Japanese arts (Karate, Judo, Jiu-Jitsu, Aikido) through Olympic Taekwondo and modern MMA. Programs like Kwon's Karate in Manhasset (Master H.Y. Kwon, 9th degree black belt), Classical Martial Arts of Long Island in Old Bethpage, and Zanshin Academy in Syosset represent decades of community-rooted martial arts education.

Martial arts builds confidence, focus, discipline, and self-defense skills in a way no team sport can replicate. Long Island's dojos welcome beginners as young as age 2 through adults of all experience levels. Browse all LI martial arts programs below.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
The best art depends on your goals. Taekwondo and karate build discipline and confidence through forms and sparring. BJJ (Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu) focuses on grappling and self-defense. Judo teaches throwing. All are excellent for focus and confidence in children.
Youth martial arts programs cost $100–$200/month including classes and belt testing fees. Many studios offer family discounts and after-school programs.
Most Long Island dojos accept students starting at age 3–4 with Little Ninjas or Tiny Tigers programs. Formal martial arts instruction typically begins at age 5–6. Black belt programs are available for teens and adults.
Research consistently shows that martial arts training improves self-confidence, emotional regulation, and conflict resolution — all of which reduce bullying victimization. Many Long Island programs specifically address bullying awareness.
Karate is a Japanese striking art emphasizing punches, kicks, and kata forms. Taekwondo is a Korean art focusing heavily on kicks and is an Olympic sport. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu focuses on ground fighting and submissions. Each has youth competitive pathways on Long Island.
