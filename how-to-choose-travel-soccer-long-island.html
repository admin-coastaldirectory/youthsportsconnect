How to Choose a
Travel Soccer Team
on Long Island

⚽ Soccer Parent Guide 10 min read · Updated 2025

Long Island has one of the most competitive youth soccer ecosystems in the country. With 97 LIJSL member clubs, thousands of travel teams, and programs ranging from recreational to MLS NEXT, finding the right fit for your child can feel overwhelming. This guide walks you through everything you need to know before making a decision.

Rec vs. Travel — Know the Difference First

Before exploring travel clubs, make sure travel soccer is actually the right move. Recreational soccer is town-based, low-cost, and focused on fun and fundamentals. Games are local, schedules are manageable, and no tryout is required. It's the right choice for most kids under 10, new players at any age, and families with schedule or budget constraints.

Travel soccer is a significant step up in commitment. Your child will tryout for a team, train with a licensed coach multiple times per week, and compete against clubs from across Long Island and neighboring states. It's the right choice when your child is showing real passion for the game, has outgrown their rec program, and your family has the schedule and budget to support it.

Key signal: If your child is consistently the most advanced player in their rec program and is asking to play more, travel soccer is probably the right next step. If they're still learning to love the game, another season of rec may serve them better.

Understanding LIJSL

The Long Island Junior Soccer League (LIJSL) is the governing body for youth travel soccer on Long Island. Established in 1966, it now serves over 60,000 players across 97 member clubs and 3,500+ teams. Nearly every travel club on Long Island competes under LIJSL.

LIJSL is affiliated with the Eastern New York Youth Soccer Association (ENYYSA) and US Youth Soccer. The league runs fall and spring travel seasons, InterLeague programs for intermediate players, and partners with the New York Red Bulls for elite player development pathways.

Within LIJSL, clubs compete in divisions based on skill level. The top teams compete in premier divisions and may also participate in ECNL (Elite Clubs National League) or MLS NEXT — the highest levels of youth soccer in the US.

Competitive Levels Explained

Long Island travel soccer has distinct tiers. Understanding where your child fits saves frustration on both ends.

  • Recreational (rec): Town-based, no tryouts, $100–300/season. Focus on fun and fundamentals.
  • InterLeague: LIJSL's intermediate option for U9+ players who want more competition than rec but aren't ready for full travel commitment.
  • Travel: Tryouts required, licensed coaches, regional competition. $800–2,000/year depending on club.
  • Elite Travel (ECNL/NPL): Top-tier club competition. Full-year training, national tournaments. $2,000–4,000+/year.
  • MLS NEXT: The highest tier. Long Island SC (LISC) is LI's MLS NEXT program. Professional-track pathway with direct connection to NY Red Bulls Academy.

Tryout Timing on Long Island

Missing tryout season is one of the most common mistakes new travel families make. Here's the LI travel soccer calendar:

  • May–June: Primary tryout window for most LIJSL clubs. This is when the bulk of roster spots are filled for the upcoming fall season.
  • Late July–August: Secondary tryouts for clubs with remaining spots. Also when some elite programs hold additional evaluations.
  • Year-round: Elite programs like LISC/MLS NEXT and some ECNL clubs hold rolling evaluations.

Pro tip: Register for tryouts in advance — most clubs require pre-registration and close the list early. Follow clubs on Instagram and check their websites starting in April. By the time tryout dates are posted publicly, spots fill up within days.

How to Actually Choose a Club

Once you've narrowed down a few clubs that match your child's level and your budget, here's how to evaluate them:

1. Watch a practice before committing

Most clubs allow parents to observe. Watch how coaches interact with players. Are they teaching, or just yelling? Is every player engaged, or are some standing around? Does the environment feel positive and developmental?

2. Talk to current club families

The best signal about a club is what current parents say when no one from the club is listening. Find parents at games or practice and ask directly: what do you love, what frustrates you, would you do it again?

3. Ask the right questions at the tryout

  • What is the training schedule — days, times, location?
  • What tournaments does this team play in?
  • How are playing time decisions made?
  • What is the coaching staff's background and licensing?
  • What happens if my child gets injured or needs to miss time?
  • What is the total cost including all fees and tournaments?
  • Is there a payment plan available?

4. Match the level to your child — not your ego

One of the most common mistakes: parents push for the highest-level club when a slightly lower level would give their child more playing time, more confidence, and more actual development. A kid who plays 80 minutes per game at a mid-level club will often develop faster than one who sits the bench at an elite club.

Red Flags to Watch For

🚩 No clear pricing up front. Legitimate clubs publish their fees. If a coach is vague about total cost, expect hidden charges.

🚩 Guaranteed roster spots before tryouts. Real clubs evaluate. Anyone promising your child a spot sight-unseen is collecting fees, not building a team.

🚩 Coaches who dismiss your questions. Good coaches welcome informed parents. Dismissiveness is a culture red flag.

🚩 No licensed coaching staff. Ask if coaches hold USSF licenses (E, D, or C License). Unlicensed coaching at the travel level is a concern.

🚩 High turnover in players or coaches. If the same club loses half its roster every year, ask why.

Notable Long Island Travel Soccer Clubs

Long Island has dozens of excellent clubs across all levels. A few well-known names:

  • Long Island SC (LISC) / MLS NEXT — The highest level on LI. Elite pathway connected to NY Red Bulls Academy.
  • Massapequa SC — Nationally recognized, strong ECNL presence, boys and girls.
  • East Meadow SC — One of LI's largest programs with broad level options.
  • Smithtown Kickers — Strong Suffolk program with excellent college placement.
  • SUSA Academy — Technical development focus, strong reputation across Nassau.
  • Island FC / The Island FC — Elite academy model, competitive at regional and national levels.

Final Advice

The best travel soccer club for your child is the one where they're challenged, growing, enjoying the game, and playing meaningful minutes. Level up when they're ready — not before. The journey from rec soccer to college is long, and burnout is real. Protect your child's love of the game first.
