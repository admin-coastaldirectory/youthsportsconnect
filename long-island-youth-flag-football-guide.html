Why Flag Football Is Exploding on Long Island

Flag football is now an Olympic sport. NFL Flag programs are in every major city. And on Long Island, enrollment in flag football leagues has surged as families look for a competitive, fun football alternative without the contact concerns of tackle.

Long Island Flag Football Programs by Area

Nassau South Shore

HTB Flag Football is the gold standard on the South Shore — 22 years running, serving Merrick, Bellmore, Wantagh, Seaford, Massapequa, and Levittown. Pre-K through 12th grade on Saturdays. No blocking, no fumbles, just football fundamentals and fun.

Nassau & Suffolk Countywide

Long Island Flag Football League (LIFFL) runs the largest adult and youth flag football operation on LI with fields across Nassau and Suffolk. NS Flag Football adds another layer of coverage with discounted early registration.

Everywhere — i9 Sports

i9 Sports runs flag football leagues across multiple LI locations. No tryouts, one practice/game per week, and everyone plays every game. Ideal for ages 3–10 just getting started.

Olympic note: Flag football will debut at the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics. College flag football programs are growing rapidly. Getting your child started now puts them years ahead of the curve.

What Age Should My Child Start Flag Football?

Most Long Island programs accept kids starting at age 4–5. At that age it's pure fun — running routes, pulling flags, and learning basic football concepts. By 8–10, kids can begin understanding offensive and defensive schemes. By middle school, serious players can compete in regional flag football tournaments.
