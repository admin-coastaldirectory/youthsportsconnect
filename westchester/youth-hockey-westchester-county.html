Youth Hockey in Westchester County

Westchester County has a deep and passionate youth ice hockey culture anchored by world-class facilities. The county is home to some of the finest ice rinks in the Northeast, and its youth programs produce players who go on to compete at prep school, junior, and college levels.

USA Hockey age divisions apply to all sanctioned programs in Westchester: Initiation (4–6), Mite (U8), Squirt (U10), Peewee (U12), Bantam (U14), Midget (U16/U18). Most Westchester rinks offer programs across all age groups.

Westchester County Ice Rinks

Westchester County has excellent ice facilities serving youth hockey. Key facilities include rinks in White Plains, Yonkers, and surrounding areas. Most rinks offer:

  • Learn to Skate USA programs (ages 3+)
  • Learn to Play Hockey clinics (ages 4–8)
  • House league programs at all age levels
  • Stick time and practice ice for teams
  • Public skating sessions

USA Hockey registration ($50–$60/year) is required for all players in sanctioned team play. Contact individual rinks for current program schedules, registration dates, and pricing.

Westchester Youth Hockey Costs

House league hockey: $1,200–$2,000 per season including ice time, officials, and league administration.

Travel hockey (competitive): $4,000–$8,000 per year in team fees, ice time, and tournament entry. Westchester travel hockey at the AAA level can exceed $10,000.

Equipment: A complete beginner setup costs $600–$1,500 (skates, helmet with cage, shoulder pads, elbow pads, shin guards, gloves, pants, stick, bag, cup/jock). Buy most gear used for first-year players — kids grow fast.

Total first-year estimate: House league: $2,500–$4,000. Travel: $6,000–$12,000+.

Frequently Asked Questions — Youth Hockey in Westchester County
What are the best youth hockey programs in Westchester County?▾
Westchester has several strong youth hockey programs at both house league and travel levels. Contact the major rinks in White Plains and Yonkers directly for current program offerings, tryout dates, and registration information. The Westchester Amateur Hockey Association (WAHA) organizes many county programs.
How much does youth hockey cost in Westchester County?▾
House league hockey in Westchester runs $1,200–$2,000 per season. Competitive travel hockey runs $4,000–$8,000+ per year. Equipment for a beginner costs $600–$1,500. Total first-year cost estimates: house league $2,500–$4,000, travel $6,000–$12,000+.
What age should my child start hockey in Westchester County?▾
Most Westchester rinks offer learn-to-skate programs starting at age 3–4. Organized hockey (Initiation/Mite level) typically starts at age 5–6. USA Hockey's ADM recommends small-area games and cross-ice formats until age 10 for optimal development.
Is there checking in Westchester youth hockey?▾
No checking is permitted below Peewee (U12) in USA Hockey sanctioned play. Mite and Squirt divisions are no-contact. Body checking is introduced at Peewee in competitive travel divisions. House league programs may have modified contact rules at Peewee level.
How do I find learn-to-skate programs in Westchester County?▾
Contact the major ice rinks in Westchester County directly for Learn to Skate USA program schedules. Programs typically run in 6–8 week sessions and include skate rental. Registration fills quickly for fall sessions — sign up in August for September starts.
