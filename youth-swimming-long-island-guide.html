Youth Competitive Swimming on Long Island
USA Swimming, LIAC & Getting Started

📍 Long Island, NY⏱ 5 min readYouthSportsConnect Editorial

Long Island's Swimming Legacy

The Long Island Aquatic Club (LIAC) is one of the most decorated USA Swimming clubs in the country, having produced Olympic swimmers and national champions. Long Island's pool infrastructure — indoor facilities at YMCAs, community centers, and private clubs across Nassau and Suffolk — supports one of the strongest youth swimming ecosystems in the Northeast.

Understanding Swim Meets

Swim meets are unlike any other youth sports competition. Your child swims 1–4 events per meet, each lasting 20–120 seconds. The goal is beating your own personal best time (PR), not just winning. Meets typically run 6–9 hours — bring food, patience, and a warm jacket for the pool deck.

The Four Strokes + IM

  • Freestyle — Most common, fastest stroke
  • Backstroke — Only stroke swum on the back
  • Breaststroke — Slowest, most technical
  • Butterfly — Most physically demanding
  • Individual Medley (IM) — All four strokes in one race

What Competitive Swimming Actually Costs

USA Swimming club membership on Long Island runs $1,200–$3,500 per year for most age group programs. LIAC's elite programs are higher. YMCA recreational team swimming is significantly less expensive and a great starting point. Factor in goggles ($15–40), suits ($30–150), swim bags, and meet entry fees when budgeting.

The two-a-day reality: Elite competitive swimmers at LIAC and comparable programs often practice twice a day (5:30am + afternoon) by age 14. This is not typical until the Senior level — most age group programs practice 5–8 hours per week. Ask any club about their practice schedule before committing.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
USA Swimming is the national governing body for competitive swimming. Join by finding a local USA Swimming member club — LIAC in Merrick, Bethpage Swim Club, Smithtown Swim Club, and Farmingdale Swim Club are among the top LI options. Attend a tryout after your child can swim one full length independently.
Summer swim teams are recreational community programs running June–August at local pools. USA Swimming is year-round competitive swimming with standardized times, national rankings, and a pathway to Olympic Trials qualifying standards.
USA Swimming club fees on Long Island run $1,200–$3,000+ per year including coaching, meet fees, and travel. Swimwear and equipment (goggles, fins, paddles, kickboard) adds $150–$300.
LIAC (Long Island Aquatic Club) based in Merrick is one of the nation's most elite competitive swimming programs. LIAC has produced dozens of Olympic Trials qualifiers, NCAA All-Americans, and USA Swimming National Team members. It is the gold standard of competitive swimming on Long Island.
USA Swimming has time standards ranging from B (entry level) through AAAA (national elite). Long Island swimmers at Metropolitan Swimming (Metro) meets compete at B/BB/A/AA levels. Reaching AA/AAA standards opens invitational meets and national-level competition.
