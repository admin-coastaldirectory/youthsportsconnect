Youth Ice Hockey on Long Island
Learn to Skate Through Elite Travel

📍 Long Island, NY⏱ 5 min readYouthSportsConnect Editorial

Long Island's Hockey Identity

The New York Islanders' four consecutive Stanley Cup championships (1980–1983) created a hockey culture on Long Island that runs deep. Youth hockey participation in Nassau and Suffolk County is among the highest per capita in New York State. Multiple indoor rinks, strong PAL programs, and travel clubs feed a pipeline of talent that regularly produces Division I college players and NHL draft picks.

Where to Start: Long Island Rinks

Iceland New Hyde Park — One of Nassau County's most active rinks. Learn-to-skate, house leagues, and figure skating year-round.

The Rinx, Hauppauge — Suffolk County's premier multi-rink facility. Youth leagues, learn-to-skate, and adult programs.

Syosset-Woodbury Ice Rink — Central Nassau location with strong youth programming and active house league.

SPORTIME Bethpage — Roller hockey on Sportcourt surface, year-round climate control. Great alternative for off-ice development.

USA Hockey Age & Equipment Requirements

All Long Island travel hockey programs affiliated with USA Hockey follow strict equipment certification standards. At the Mite level (6–8), helmets require a HECC sticker. Full cage is mandatory through Peewee (age 12). Shoulder pads, elbow pads, shin guards, gloves, and an athletic cup are required at all levels. Budget $400–$800 for a complete starter equipment set.

Cost reality: Youth ice hockey is among the most expensive youth sports. Travel hockey on Long Island typically costs $2,500–$6,000 per year including ice time, equipment, and tournament fees. House league hockey runs $300–$800 per season at most LI rinks.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Start with a Learn to Skate program at a local arena. Nassau County arenas include Newbridge Road Park (Bellmore), Cold Spring Harbor, and Syosset. Once your child can skate independently, enroll in USA Hockey's Initiation Program (IP) for ages 4–9.
Ice hockey is one of the most expensive youth sports. Equipment alone costs $500–$1,500. Annual coaching, ice time, and tournament fees for travel programs run $3,000–$8,000+. House league programs are more affordable at $800–$2,000/year.
House hockey is recreational, skill-based, and plays games only at home rinks. Travel hockey is competitive, tryout-based, and plays against teams across Long Island, the metro area, and regionally. Travel involves significantly more ice time and cost.
Long Island Jr Gulls (LIJG) is the premier AAA youth hockey organization on Long Island. LIJG has produced numerous NHL players and NCAA Division I athletes. It is the highest level of youth hockey development on Long Island.
Key arenas include Bethpage Ice (Nassau), Syosset Ice, Cold Spring Harbor Ice, Newbridge Road Park Arena (Bellmore), Hauppauge, LICAC (Northport), and Cantiague Park. Each runs youth programs ranging from Learn to Skate through competitive travel.
