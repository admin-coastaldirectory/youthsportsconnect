U6 Youth Sports on Long Island
Ages 4–6

U6 is the youngest age division in most Long Island youth sports — typically covering kids ages 4, 5, and 6. At this stage, the focus is entirely on fun, motor skill development, and introducing kids to sport basics. No keeping score, no cutting — just play.

Complete Guide

What is U6 Youth Sports?

U6 stands for 'Under 6' — meaning players must be under 6 years old (typically by April 30 of the season year, though cutoff dates vary by sport and league). Most leagues group U6 with 4 and 5 year olds as well, creating a U4/U5/U6 or 'Micro' division.

At U6, games use modified rules: smaller fields, lighter balls, no goalkeepers in soccer, no pitching machines in baseball (T-ball only), and no formal score-keeping. The entire philosophy is developmental play.

Most U6 programs on Long Island run in the fall (September–November) or spring (March–June). Sessions are typically 45–60 minutes once per week including a short practice followed by a mini-game.

U6 Sports Available on Long Island

Soccer U6 (ages 4–6): The most popular U6 sport on LI. Programs like LIJSL, local PAL leagues, i9 Sports, Soccer Stars, and Lil Athletes all offer U6 divisions. Expect 4v4 games on small fields with no goalkeeper.

Baseball T-Ball U6: Most town rec programs offer T-ball for ages 5–6. Players hit off a stationary tee, no pitching. Nassau PAL and Suffolk PAL both offer T-ball. Registration typically opens January–February for spring season.

Lacrosse U6: Nassau/Suffolk PAL Lacrosse offers a U6 'Micro Lax' division with foam balls and tiny sticks. Many town PAL programs offer it. Great introduction to the sport LI is famous for.

Basketball U6: Rec programs through town parks departments, YMCA, i9 Sports, and PAL offer U6 basketball with low baskets (6–7 ft) and smaller balls. Nassau and Suffolk both have strong youth basketball rec programs.

Gymnastics (Ages 3–6): Most gymnastics gyms on LI offer beginner programs for ages 3 and up. No 'U6 division' per se — gymnastic programs use ability levels rather than age cutoffs.

Flag Football U6: NFL FLAG programs are available in Nassau and Suffolk for ages 5+. No contact — pure fun with flags.

U6 Season Dates and Registration on Long Island

Fall Season: Registration typically opens July–August. Season runs September–November. Most U6 programs run 8–10 weeks.

Spring Season: Registration opens January–February. Season runs March–June. Spring is the most popular season for U6 soccer and baseball on LI.

Year-Round Options: Gymnastics, basketball clinics, and some soccer academy programs run year-round indoors.

Cost: Most rec U6 programs on Long Island cost $75–$165 per season including uniform. Private academy programs may cost $150–$300.

How to Choose a U6 Program

Proximity is everything at this age. Kids 4–6 have limited attention spans — a 45-minute drive each way will exhaust everyone before the first game. Choose the closest quality program first.

Rec over travel: No 4-year-old needs a travel team. U6 is 100% recreational. Anyone offering 'elite' programs at U6 is marketing to parents, not serving kids.

Coaching matters more than league. A patient, energetic coach who makes it fun is more valuable than the most competitive league on LI at this age.

Frequently Asked Questions — U6 Youth Sports on Long Island
What age is U6 in youth sports?▾
U6 means 'under 6 years old.' Most LI leagues use April 30 as the age cutoff — so if your child is under 6 on April 30 of the season year, they are eligible for U6. Many leagues also accept 4 and 5 year olds in U6 divisions.
Is U6 soccer competitive on Long Island?▾
No — U6 soccer on Long Island is entirely recreational and developmental. Games use modified rules (4v4, small fields, no goalkeeper, no score-keeping). The focus is fun and motor skill development, not competition.
When does U6 sports registration open on Long Island?▾
Spring season registration typically opens January–February. Fall season registration opens July–August. PAL programs, town rec departments, and i9 Sports all have different opening dates — check each program directly.
How much does U6 youth sports cost on Long Island?▾
Most rec U6 programs cost $75–$165 per season including a uniform. Private academy programs (Soccer Stars, Lil Athletes, similar) typically cost $150–$300 per session or season.
What is the best U6 sport to start with on Long Island?▾
Soccer and baseball/t-ball are the most popular U6 entry points on Long Island. Soccer is especially popular in Nassau County. Lacrosse is also a great LI-specific introduction given the area's strong lacrosse culture.
